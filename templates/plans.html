My Plans for the Naval Academy

January 15th, 2025

In regards to my plans for the United States Naval Academy, I hold many dreams of excitement to come. Though I'm certain these ideas are miniscule in the eyes of God, they are grand in my own, and I eagerly share them with you today. I will cover my intended majors, extracurriculars, and planned career steps thus far.

As many of you are already aware, I maintain a particular inclination towards cybersecurity, and as such I plan to double major at USNA in both Computer Science and Cyber Operations. Fundamentally, this will forge the pathway to gaining high-paying positions and provide me with an aire of transferability throughout the tech-domain. Having such degrees on my resume will likely bring access to any career of my choice (even after the military).

For extracurricular activities, I am pleased to present a high-level overview of the undertakings I am preparing.

Wrestling was a difficult consideration of mine, and to be entirely honest, I'm still not certain of my decision... Tentatively I have decided not to wrestle at USNA. I find that it's not truly something that makes me happy or brings me excitement and fulfillment. As such I deem it unworthy of my time and have abandoned its cruel hand to those who love it with greater ferocity than I do.

As for other extracurricular activities, I plan to lift every day, as lifting brings me joy and fulfillment. I also plan to partake in jiu jitsu at USNA. Maybe even boxing or judo on occasion. I do so love these practical martial arts. They make me more dangerous and further equipped to defend my family and my country. As such they are highly significant in my eyes and worthy of pursuit.

And I hold yet one final desire for the United States Naval Academy: to create my own club. Yes, this will be quite a challenge. I searched for hacking clubs at the academy and found none, both to my disappointment and to my pleasure. Lord willing, I will create such a club, where anyone can go to learn more about penetration testing, digital security, and cyber analysis. We will discuss the latest exploits, craft our own, hold hacking competitions (CTFs, Revd vs. Blue, Hackathons, etc.), and even do some bug bounty together. Cybersecurity made accessible and fun.

In the end, these plans rest in the hands of God and are subject to change. But as of now, these are my desired undertakings for my time at the United States Naval Academy.

Dirk Rumfelt
